# Medical and Research Disclaimer

Gene Prioritizer AI is research and educational software under active development. It is **not a medical device**,
not medical advice, does not diagnose disease, and does not provide treatment recommendations or
a substitute for professional judgment.

Do not use this software for emergency, clinical, reproductive, or other patient-care decisions.
Any future output may be incomplete, outdated, incorrect, or affected by source-data bias and must
be independently reviewed by appropriately qualified professionals against authoritative sources.

Gene validation confirms nomenclature only. An approved HGNC symbol does not diagnose disease,
establish gene-disease causality, indicate pathogenicity, or replace professional interpretation.

Deterministic ranking scores are prioritization scores for review. They are not diagnostic
probabilities, risk estimates, clinical interpretations, or evidence that a gene causes a condition.

Free-text extraction returns suggested HPO terms only. Suggested terms may be incomplete,
incorrect, negated, uncertain, or family-history-only, and must be reviewed and confirmed before
ranking or interpretation.

PubMed literature evidence is citation support only. Publication count does not prove gene-disease
causality, clinical validity, pathogenicity, diagnosis, or treatment relevance. Absence of PubMed
results does not rule out a gene. Every citation and interpretation requires expert review against
authoritative sources.

Do not enter real patient data, protected health information, personally identifiable information,
or confidential case material. PubMed enrichment is not diagnostic capability.

The Phase 8 web UI displays this disclaimer in the safety banner, informational pages, ranking
result context, gene detail view, and generated Markdown/JSON reports. CSV exports include ranked
data only and should be interpreted alongside the disclaimer.

Phase 9 security controls reduce deployment risk but do not change the research-use scope. Security
headers, rate limiting, admin controls, audit logs, and CSP do not make the application a medical
device, diagnostic system, HIPAA-compliant service, or source of clinical recommendations.

If licensed GeneCards/GeneALaCart annotations are imported in Phase 10, they remain
user-provided licensed annotations only. They are not diagnostic evidence, not model-training data,
not a substitute for authoritative review, and not proof of gene-disease causality. The deployment
operator or uploading admin is responsible for confirming licensing rights and for excluding patient
or confidential data from uploaded files.
